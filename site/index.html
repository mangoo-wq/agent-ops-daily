This week in agent ops

signal #1

Quiet-first is winning

The strongest trust warning still sounds boring: too many low-value pings. Attention is the scarce resource, so silence is starting to look like competence.

signal #2

Approval theater is real

Operators are moving past “ask before acting” as a blanket rule. The real conversation is now about authority boundaries, reversibility, and when approvals become rubber stamps.

signal #3

Outcome metrics beat activity metrics

The language is shifting away from raw output counts toward acted-on rate, regret rate, and other measures that tell you whether the human world actually changed.

What people are actually measuring

what people are measuring

Metric of the week: acted-on rate

Operators keep returning to a simple question: did the human actually do anything after the agent spoke? It is a cleaner trust metric than raw output volume.

what people are measuring

Rollback / regret rate

Useful because it captures visible correction. If a human keeps undoing or overriding actions, trust is already leaking.

what people are measuring

PTA ratio

A strong authority metric: how much work happens within trusted bounds vs. how much still needs permission friction.
